Solkadhi | kokam kadhi | Kadhi | Maharastrian recipe

Solkadhi or a digestive kadhi made of kokum is very refreshing and tasty drink, a speciality of Konkan region that extends from Goa through Maharashtra. The kokum fruit is largely produced in this Konkan region and used as an astringent or souring agent in many of the specialties of the area. This Solkadhi uses coconut along with kokum and the drink is either had with rice or consumed as a digestive beverage at the end of a meal.

Ingredients

  • Chopped coconut
  • 2-3 Green chillies
  • 1 Inch Ginger piece
  • 5-6 garlic cloves
  • 1/4 Cup Kokum extract
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander

Procedure

Solkadhi serves in restaurants of kokan region, it tastes nice. It has sour, sweet taste. As it contains kokum extract and coconut milk together. It is way easy to prepare. Here I show how to make it.

Take fresh coconut, chopped into pieces, using knife. Now take grinding jar, add pieces of fresh coconut, 2-3 green chillies, 1 Inch of ginger, 5-6 garlic cloves. Grind them together to fine paste. Add water while grinding. Grind rest of coconut pieces separately then mix all mixture together. Then take a cotton cloth or strainer and extract the milk from grinded mixture. Add water while extracting coconut milk.

Once Milk gets extracted add 1/4 Th cup of kokum extract, also called as kokum aagal in Marathi. It has strong sour taste. Add 1/2 Tbs salt to it. Stir solkadhi nicely. Garnish with some freshly chopped coriander. It tastes great when it gets cool down.

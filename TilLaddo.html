Til Laddo | Tilgul | Makarsankranti Special Recipe

Tilgul is a colourful sesame candy coated with sesame seeds; in Maharashtra people exchange tilgul on Sankranti. A Hindu festival celebrated on 14 January. Due to Sesame and Jaggery, this candy is healthy for human body during winter season. That's why this candy exchange festival is in winter.

The sweet is a mixture of sesame seeds called "Til" in Marathi and jaggery called "Gul" in Marathi and hence the name. On Sankranti eve, Families serve their guests with Tilgul while saying "Tilgul ghya, goad goad bola" which literally means "Take Tilgul and talk sweetly".
Here is easy and simple recipe to make this crispy delicious laddos in 10 minutes. Do try this and let your reactions know in comment section below.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups white sesame seeds
  • 1 Cup Jaggery
  • 1/2 Cup Roasted crushed Peanuts
  • 1 Tbs Ghee

Procedure

As Makarsankranti is one of the biggest festivals of Maharashtrian, they celebrate it with joy and happiness. The special sweet which made on this occasion is Tilgul. Also called as Til laddo, which are made from white sesame seeds and jaggery.

Take 2 Cups of White sesame seeds and roast it in pan for 2-3 minutes on medium flame. Once Sesame seeds get roasted evenly take out from Pan. Roast 1/2 Cup of Peanuts evenly, and separate skin from it. Now Add 1 Cup of grated jaggery in same pan, add 1 Tbs ghee into it. Once jaggery get melts down completely add roasted sesame seeds and peanuts into it. Mix properly. Put some ghee on your palms and take small portion of mixture and roll it into ladoo. Repeat the same procedure. Once all til ladoo get ready you can preserve them in container. It can last for month.

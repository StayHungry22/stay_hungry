Rasgulla | Rashogulla Recipe | With homemade Chenna

Rasgulla refers to a syrupy dessert made from chena (cottage cheese), and sugar. It is a popular dessert in eastern parts of India, especially West Bengal and Orissa. It is prepared by kneading chena (fresh curd cheese) often lightened with a small amount of semolina and rolling them into small balls. These balls are then simmered in a sugar syrup. While serving add a drop of rose water (only organic and edible type of rose water, not rose perfume or synthetic flavour’s) to enhance the flavour and taste.

Ingredients

  • 1 litre Milk
  • 2 Tbs Vinegar
  • 5-6 Saffron strands
  • 1.5 Cup Sugar

Procedure

For preparing Rasgulla’s you can use any method either, making from paneer (Prepared chena) or by curdling the fresh milk. Here I have use second method. First boil half litre of milk in saucepan. Boil until it gets warm. Don’t boil milk completely. Then take 1 Tbs of vinegar in bowl, add 1/4 Cup water to it. Dilute the Vinegar mixture. Then add diluted mixture in batches, until milks start curdling.

Take drainer or cloth and drain the milk mixture on it. Take out excess water from chena. Now tie the chena in cloth and hang it down for 2-3 Hours. Once chena get prepared. Start making sugar syrup for rasgulla’s. Take 1.5 Cup of sugar in pan, add 2 Cups of water to it. Keep stirring until sugar get melts completely. Add 5-6 saffron strands to sugar, for colouring and flavour.

Now take out chena from cloth and start kneading it. Knead for 4-5 Minutes. You can add semolina for better binding. Now take out small portion of chena and roll then into a round shaped ball. Make sure ball should be crack free. Once all balls get ready simmer them in sugar syrup and cook them for 10-15 minutes on low-medium flame. If sugar syrup gets reduced, then add 3-4 tbs of water in between.

It takes around 20 Minutes to cook. And you can see the difference in size of balls. They turned soft and large. Take out prepared rasgulla’s in bowl and keep them in freeze for 2-3 hours. So, they absorb the sugar syrup nicely. And your homemade rasgulla’s are ready to serve.

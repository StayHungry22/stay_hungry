Chole Masala | Chana Chole | Punjabi Chole Recipe

Chana masala also known as channay, chole masala, chhole masala, chole or chholay (plural), is a dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. The main ingredient is a variety of chickpea called chana or kala chana ('black chana'). They are half the diameter of typical chickpeas with a stronger flavour and firmer texture even after being cooked.
Chole is the name for the larger and lighter coloured chickpea commonly found in the West. These are known as kabuli chana in Hindi. Chana masala is fairly dry and spicy with a sour citrus note (the flavor usually comes from coriander and onion). Chana are usually replaced by chole in most restaurants,[clarification needed] and both versions are widely sold as snack food and street food in the Indian subcontinent.

Along with chickpeas, the ingredients of chana masala typically include onion, chopped tomatoes, ghee, cumin, turmeric, coriander powder, garlic, chillies, ginger, amchoor or lemon juice, and garam masala.
Chole masala is a flavorful and delicious Indian curry made by cooking chickpeas in a spicy onion, tomato gravy. It can be eaten with rice, roti, naan, poori or bhature. If you don't like chunky curries then you will love this.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups Chickpeas
  • 2 onions
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2 Tbs oil
  • 1 Bay leaf, cinnamon, 2 cloves
  • 2 Black pepper, 1 Cardamom
  • 1/2 Tbs cumin
  • 1/4 Tbs Hing (Asafoetida)
  • 1 Tbs Ginger garlic paste
  • 1 Tbs Red chilli powder
  • 1/2 Tbs turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 Tbs Garam Masala
  • 1.5 Tbs Chole masala
  • Orange food colour (Optional)
  • Coriander

Procedure

For preparing chole masala dish you have to soak chickpeas before preparing it. The chickpeas have to soak for 7-8 hours. So its way easy to cook. For soaking 2 cups of kaboli chana 2 or 2.5 cups of water is enough.
For Rich gravy base grind 2 chopped onions and 1 tomato separately. Make puree of it. Now add 2-3 Tbs of oil in pressure cooker, now add all dry spices like bay leaf, cardamom, 2-3 cloves, piece of cinaamon, 2-3 black pepper corns, 1/2 Tbs cumin seeds and pinch of Hing (Asafoetida). Roast nicely. Then Add Onion paste cook it for 2-3 minutes. Add 1 Tbs of ginger-garlic paste after that add tomato puree and cook it on low flame for more 2 minutes. Once everything get mixed together add other spices like Red chilli powder according to your spiciness, 1/2 Tbs turmeric, garam masala, and chole masala. For a rich look to gravy you can add pinch of orange food colour into it. Get all mixed together nicely. Then add soaked chickpeas into cooker. And salt to taste. Add 1 cup of water and then close lid of pressure cooker. Cook for 4-5 whistles. Once cooker get cool down take out prepared chole masala into bowl and garnish with freshly chopped coriander.
And restaurant style Thick gravy chole masala is ready to serve.

Chakli Recipe | Diwali snacks | Fried Snack

Chakli, chakkuli, Karnataka origin is a savoury snack. It is a spiral shaped snack with a spiked surface. Chakri is typically made from flours of rice, bengal gram (brown chickpea) and black gram (urad daal). It has several variations, depending on the types and proportion of flours used. Murukku, a similar snack typically made without the Bengal gram flour, is also sometimes called "chakli".

Chakli, a spiral shaped crispy deep fried snack, is one of the traditional Indian snack item enojoyed during festivals like Diwali. This snack is known with different names in different parts of India and also prepared with different base ingredients. I made chakli from all-purpose flour(Maida) and Moong Dal.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup All-purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 1/4 Cup Moong Dal
  • 1 Tbs Green chilli Paste
  • 1 Tbs Jeera
  • 2 Tbs Sesame seeeds
  • 1/2 Tbs Turmeric
  • 1/2 Tbs salt

Procedure

Steam the 1/4 th cup moong dal with half cup water in pressure cooker. Tie a cup of plain flour in cloth and steam it along with dal for 2-3 Whistels. Once flour get cool down, crumble the flour and sieve it. Now churn the cooked dal properly so there will no lumps occurred. Add Turmeric, Green chilli paste, salt and Cumin seeds. Mix all the mixture nicely. Bind all together, it turns into a dough texture. To get spiked surface rounded, proper chakli I used chakli machine here. Fill some dough in chakli machine and prepare chakli’s on kitchen platform. Heat oil for deep frying chakli on medium flame. Once all done. Fry chakli’s one by one until it turns golden in colour. This can be eaten as snacks or you can make it on festive seasons also.

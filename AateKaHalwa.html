Aata Halwa | Kada Prasad recipe | Halwa Recipe

Wheat halwa or Kada Prasad made from whole wheat flour or atta is a popular sweet made in north India. This atte ka halwa recipe is a Tasty melt in the mouth halwa and taste too good. the recipe is easy to make and can be made for any occasion or any day. It could be celebration, a festival or when you just want to have something sweet. the recipe has a strong resemblance to the khara prasad or kada prasad offered in punjabi gurudwara langars but is quite different.But in karah prashad preparation the same proportion (quantity) of flour, ghee and sugar is used.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup Wheat flour
  • 1/2 Cup Ghee
  • 1/2 Cup Sugar
  • 1/4 Cup Milk
  • 1 Tbs Cardamom powder
  • Cashew nuts, Raisins

Procedure

Aate ka Halwa is usually served as Prasad in gurudwara, and at other religious places also. It is made with wheat flour. It contain lot of ghee to make it more rich and smooth in texture.
First heat about 1/2 Cup of ghee in pan. Then add a cup of wheat flour into it. Oast flour evenly for 5-6 minutes. Stir the mixture continuously. Once it get roasted add 1 cup of water and 1/2 Cup of milk into it. Mix the mixture slowly the mixture starts binding, then add 1/2 cup sugar (Increase as per your taste). Again stir the mixture nicely.For enhancing the taste of Halwa add 1 Tbs of cardamom powder into it. You can add dry fruits of your choice. But make sure that they should be roasted before. After adding dry fruits your halwa is ready to serve.

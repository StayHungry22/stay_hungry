Gajar Halwa Recipe| Carrot Halwa | Sweet Dish

Gajar ka halwa, also known as gajorer halua, gajrela, gajar pak, and carrot halwa is a carrot-based sweet dessert pudding from the Indian subcontinent. It is made by placing grated carrots in a pot containing a specific amount of water, milk and sugar, cardamom and then cooking while stirring regularly. It is often served with a garnish of almonds and pistachios. The nuts and other items used are first sautéed in ghee, a type of clarified butter from the Indian subcontinent.

The dessert is traditionally eaten during all the festivals in India, mainly on the occasion of Diwali, Holi, Eid al-Fitr and Raksha Bandhan. It is served hot during the winter. It is a light nutritious dessert with less fat.

Ingredients

  • 500 Gms Grated Carrots
  • 1/4 Cup Ghee
  • 1/2 Cup Sugar
  • 1/4 Cup Milk
  • 2 Tbs Milk Powder
  • 1 Tbs Cardamom powder
  • Chopped Dry fruits

Procedure

Gajar halwa is one of loved sweet dish over India. It is pretty simple to make. First put pan on flame add 500 Gms of grated carrots into pan. Cook it for 2 minutes on low flame. Then add 2 Tbs Ghee into it. Stir continuously while cooking. Then add 1/2 Cup sugar, you can change measurements according to your sweetness. Once sugar get melt completely, add 1/2 Cup milk, you can replace milk by using 2 Tbs milk powder or khoya. It gives a rich taste to halwa. Get mixed all ingredients together. Close the lid and cook for more 4-5 minutes. Then garnish it with sautéed dry fruits and nuts.
Even you can make nice textured barfi out of it. Just put by halwa in tray and freeze it for an hour. Cut into square pieces. And Carrot barfi is ready.
